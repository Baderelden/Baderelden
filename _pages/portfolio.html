My research interests lie at the intersection of Machine Learning (ML), Data Mining, Combinatorial Optimisation, and Genetic Programming. I bring a wealth of experience in working with various industries, fostering the adoption and development of applied machine learning techniques. This experience includes multiple industrial Knowledge Transfer Partnerships, where I've been actively involved in bridging the gap between academia and industry. 

One primary research focus of mine is the creation and application of ML methodologies for early outcome prediction in real-world scenarios. Notably, I'm engaged in enhancing the utilization of data from Intensive Care Units and the digital marketing sector. These pursuits aim to facilitate rapid and accurate predictions, thereby improving healthcare outcomes and marketing efficiency. A critical problem that draws my attention in ML is class imbalance in data sets. I am keenly invested in pioneering techniques to alleviate this issue, aiming to optimize ML models' performance when dealing with imbalanced classes. 

In addition, I hold a keen interest in hyper-heuristics and genetic programming, specifically in the realm of automatic heuristic design. This interest dovetails with my commitment to advancing the automation capabilities within the field of machine learning, driving efficiency and innovation in problem-solving. 

Digital Marketing and Consumer Behaviour Prediction: This research probes the confluence of digital marketing and predictive analytics. Using Contextual Multi-Armed Bandits algorithms, we aim to effectively predict consumer behaviour, optimise marketing strategies, and provide personalised customer experiences in real-time, thereby enhancing business performance and customer satisfaction.
Machine Learning for Early Outcome Prediction in ICU: In this research, machine learning is employed in the Intensive Care Unit with the objective of predicting patient outcomes at an early stage. We analyse varied data points, such as vital signs and medical history, to empower healthcare professionals to make swift and informed decisions that could enhance patient prognosis and potentially save lives.
Hyper-Heuristics: This research delves into hyper-heuristics, exploring the use of high-level problem-solving frameworks that produce strategies to solve a wide range of challenging problems. The focus is on improving computational efficiency, flexibility, and adaptability in various fields, from optimisation to machine learning.
Class Imbalance and Biased Random Forest: This research addresses the issue of class imbalance in machine learning, a prevalent problem that can bias predictive models and impair their performance. By developing and refining Biased Random Forest methods, we aim to improve the robustness and accuracy of predictive models, ensuring they deliver reliable insights across diverse datasets.
Failure Detection in Industry and Electronic Storage Systems: This research entails the application of predictive analytics to pre-emptively identify potential failures in industrial and electronic storage systems. By leveraging machine learning techniques, we strive to reduce system downtime, increase operational efficiency, and mitigate the risk of unanticipated equipment breakdowns.
